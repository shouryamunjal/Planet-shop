Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in our solar system, known for its striking deep blue color, which results from the absorption of red light by methane in its atmosphere.
It is a gas giant with a dynamic atmosphere, featuring strong winds and storms, including the Great Dark Spot, similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot. Neptune has 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest, and is also known for its faint ring system.
