Venus

Venus, the second planet from the Sun, is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to their similar size and composition.
However, Venus is much hotter, with surface temperatures reaching 475°C (900°F) because of a thick atmosphere rich in carbon dioxide, creating a runaway greenhouse effect. Its dense clouds are primarily sulfuric acid, and the planet rotates slowly in the opposite direction of most planets. Venus has no moons or rings.
